Tacos al pastor

Description

Tacos al pastor is a mouthwatering Mexican street food, featuring thinly sliced marinated pork cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The vibrant adobo marinade infuses the meat with smoky, sweet, and tangy flavors, while the slow roasting creates tender, crispy edges. Served on soft corn tortillas with fresh cilantro, diced onions, and juicy pineapple, each bite is an explosion of deliciousness that captures the essence of Mexico's rich culinary heritage.

To make authentic tacos al pastor, begin by preparing the flavorful adobo marinade. Combine dried chilies, achiote paste, vinegar, garlic, Mexican oregano, cumin, and other spices in a blender, creating a vibrant red paste. Next, thinly slice pork shoulder or pork butt and coat it generously with the adobo, ensuring each piece is well-marinated. Allow the pork to marinate for several hours or preferably overnight to enhance the flavors. When ready to cook, stack the marinated pork on a vertical rotisserie, allowing it to slow-roast and caramelize to perfection. As the meat cooks, the tantalizing aroma will fill the air. Once done, thinly slice the juicy, tender pork off the spit and serve on warm corn tortillas, topped with fresh cilantro, diced onions, and juicy pineapple chunks. Add a squeeze of lime juice and your favorite salsa for the ultimate explosion of tastes and textures that make tacos al pastor an irresistible culinary delight.

Ingredients

  • Pork shoulder or pork butt, thinly sliced
  • Dried chilies (guajillo, ancho, or a mix of both), deseeded and stemmed
  • Achiote paste
  • White vinegar
  • Garlic cloves
  • Mexican oregano
  • Ground cumin
  • Ground cloves
  • Ground cinnamon
  • Pineapple juice (or fresh pineapple)
  • Salt and pepper
  • Corn tortillas
  • Fresh cilantro, chopped
  • White onions, finely diced
  • Fresh pineapple, cut into small chunks
  • Lime wedges
  • Salsa or salsa verde (optional, for serving)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Adobo Marinade:
    1. Deseed and stem the dried chilies (guajillo, ancho, or a mix).
    2. In a blender, combine the dried chilies, achiote paste, white vinegar, garlic cloves, Mexican oregano, ground cumin, ground cloves, ground cinnamon, pineapple juice (or fresh pineapple), salt, and pepper.
    3. Blend until you get a smooth, vibrant red paste.
  2. Marinate the Pork:
    1. Thinly slice the pork shoulder or pork butt.
    2. Coat the pork slices generously with the adobo marinade, ensuring each piece is well-covered.
    3. Place the marinated pork in a container, cover it, and refrigerate for several hours or preferably overnight to allow the flavors to infuse.
  3. Prepare the Toppings:
    1. Chop fresh cilantro and finely dice white onions.
    2. Cut fresh pineapple into small chunks.
    3. Cut lime into wedges for serving.
  4. Cook the Pork:
    1. Preheat your grill or oven to medium-high heat.
    2. If using a vertical rotisserie, stack the marinated pork on the skewer.
    3. Slow-roast the pork, allowing it to cook and caramelize until tender and crispy at the edges. If using an oven, you can roast the pork on a baking sheet.
  5. Warm the Tortillas:
    1. Heat the corn tortillas on a griddle or in a dry pan over medium heat until they are soft and pliable.
  6. Assemble the Tacos:
    1. Take a warm tortilla and add a few slices of the cooked pork.
    2. Top the pork with chopped cilantro, diced onions, and pineapple chunks.
  7. Serve:
    1. Serve the tacos al pastor hot and fresh, accompanied by lime wedges for squeezing and salsa or salsa verde on the side, if desired.
