Pozole

Description

Red pozole is a traditional Mexican soup renowned for its rich and hearty flavors. This vibrant and enticing dish features tender hominy, slow-cooked pork or chicken, and an array of spices, all simmered in a savory and fiery red chili broth. The bright red hue comes from a blend of dried guajillo, ancho, and other chilies, infusing the broth with a delightful depth and smoky undertones. Garnished with fresh lime, shredded lettuce, diced onions, radishes, and chopped cilantro, red pozole offers a tantalizing mix of textures and a burst of zest with every spoonful. A cherished symbol of Mexican culinary heritage, it brings warmth and comfort to gatherings, celebrations, and family meals.

Ingredients

  • Hominy (large white corn kernels)
  • Pork or chicken (cut into chunks)
  • Dried guajillo chilies
  • Dried ancho chilies
  • Onion (chopped)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Chicken or vegetable broth
  • Mexican oregano
  • Cumin
  • Bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh lime wedges
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Diced onions
  • Radishes (thinly sliced)
  • Chopped cilantro

Steps

  1. Soak the dried guajillo and ancho chilies in hot water for about 15-20 minutes until they become soft.
  2. Remove the stems and seeds from the rehydrated chilies, then place them in a blender.
  3. Add chopped onion, minced garlic, Mexican oregano, cumin, salt, and pepper to the blender.
  4. Puree the ingredients until you have a smooth red chili sauce.
  5. In a large pot, combine the pork or chicken chunks with the red chili sauce and cook over medium heat for a few minutes.
  6. Add the hominy and enough chicken or vegetable broth to cover all the ingredients.
  7. Drop in a couple of bay leaves for extra flavor.
  8. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover the pot, and let it simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until the meat is tender and flavors are well combined.
  9. While the pozole is simmering, prepare the garnishes: slice the radishes, dice the onions, and chop the cilantro.
  10. Once the pozole is ready, remove the bay leaves and serve it hot in bowls.
  11. Allow everyone to customize their bowls with garnishes and fresh lime wedges on the side.
